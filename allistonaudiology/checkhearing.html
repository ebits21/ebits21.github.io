Checking for Hearing Loss

Three types of hearing checks are available:

  1. Comprehensive Assessment,
  2. Basic Test
  3. and Hearing Screening.

We recommend a Comprehensive Assessment. Basic testing has been available and unchanged since the 1980s and is not recommended. Hearing Screening can determine if a comprehensive assessment is warranted.

It is recommended that people over fifty years of age get their hearing checked regularly.

COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT

Your audiologist will begin with a core set of tests:

  • Video inspection of the ear (video otoscopy)
  • Eardrum movement testing (tympanometry and middle ear muscle reflex testing)
  • Puretone testing at up to 10 tones (audiometry)
  • Speech testing (speech audiometry)

Depending on initial results, your Audiologist may select further relevant tests including:

  • Inner ear hair cell testing (otoacoustic emission test)
  • High frequency audiometry (9000 to 16000 Hz for chemotherapy, radiation therapy monitoring)
  • Speech-in-noise testing
  • Tinnitus testing (tinnitus matching)

and others.

BASIC TEST

A basic hearing test only includes the following:

  • Looking into your ear (otoscopy)
  • Eardrum movement testing (tympanometry)
  • Puretone testing, often at about 6 tones (audiometry)
  • and speech testing (speech audiometry)

SCREENING

Hearing screenings are generally performed at work or in the community and generally use a pass/fail criteria. For example, if your hearing level was lower than a set level, you would be referred for a comprehensive assessment.
